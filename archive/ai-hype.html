On creative blocks and other myths

Published: June 2025

Everyone talks about creative blocks like they're some mystical force that descends upon artists without warning. Like we're helpless victims of our own minds.

I call bullshit.

The myth of inspiration

Here's what I've learned after years of making videos: creativity isn't about waiting for lightning to strike. It's about showing up every day, even when—especially when—you don't feel like it.

The best work I've ever done came from the days I felt least inspired. The days when I had to drag myself to the editing suite, when every cut felt wrong, when I questioned why I was doing this at all.

Resistance is the compass

Steven Pressfield was right about resistance. The more resistance you feel toward a project, the more important it probably is. That documentary I've been avoiding? The one that makes my stomach turn when I think about it? That's exactly the one I need to make.

Creative blocks aren't blocks at all. They're signposts pointing toward the work that matters most.

The practice

So here's my practice: I show up. Every day. Even if it's just for ten minutes. Even if I delete everything I create. The act of showing up is the work.

The muse is a myth. The work is real.
